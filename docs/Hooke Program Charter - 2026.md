# 2026 Product Profiles

### Program Overview:

#### **Current Status:**

- We can simulate phenomic responses to compound perturbations in new cell types (\~65% precision across 20 cell types in proof-of-principle studies).  
- We can simulate transcriptomic responses for unseen compounds in new cellular contexts (\>90% retrieval for Hooke-Tx vs 69% experimental reproducibility on OOD test sets).

Together, these results demonstrate that it’s possible to generalize across perturbations and cellular contexts in both morphological and gene spaces.

#### **Next Steps:**

Model improvement and pilot evaluations on **genetic, compound, and combination perturbations** to support:

* Program initiation & nomination  
* Target ID / deconvolution  
* Hit-to-Lead (H2L) / Lead Optimization (LO)

In particular, our immediate next step is to launch a retrospective study on GI-ONC data to : 

* demonstrate that Hooke could predict equivalent target and compound therapeutic insights at \<50% of experimental cost.   
* Virtually screen \~1M novel compounds for GI-ONC as potential partnership assets.

### Opportunity:

Accelerate hit & target discovery/confirmation across broader disease-relevant cellular contexts without increasing experimental costs. Why now? We have scalable algorithms that enable this and the compute & data to achieve it.

Replace late, expensive transcriptomics and tox decision points with actionable virtual readouts that front-load risk and clarify mechanism (pathways, MoA, etc)

### Problem:

Our experimental platforms are powerful but capacity-limited and combinatorially unscalable:

* Phenomics cannot scale across compounds × doses × disease contexts × timepoints, limiting hypothesis generation for program initiation and nomination.  
* TrekSeq and follow-up transcriptomic assays are throughput- and cost-constrained, often occurring late in programs, delaying hit confirmation and mechanistic validation and driving premature chemistry investment.

As a result, we are constrained in the number and quality of hypotheses we can generate and test to replenish our internal and partnership pipelines.

### Impact:

Hooke-1 will simulate phenotypic experiments which will drive model-first discoveries of biological insights, making Recursion better at drug discovery because we will fill the gaps in our pre-clinical pipeline by enabling more translatable program decisions. This work is going to impact our internal pipeline and partnership team (Value \+ OS) and more specifically these processes:

- Map building for partnership and internal use  
- Program initiation and nomination  
- Target ID/deconvolution (using phenomics and transcriptomics simulation)  
- Hit-ID and confirmation (using phenomics simulation)  
- Lead Opt and mechanistic clarity (using transcriptomics simulation) 

### Our Approach:

	

| Inputs | Outputs |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Training:** All phenomics data (cell paint & brightfield) and perturbational transcriptomics signatures (internal TrekSeq \+ PerturbSeq, public transcriptomics data), perturbation representations (chemical & genetic) | Simulated lab measurements for novel therapeutic context  in morphological  (e.g. pixels / Phenom embeddings) and gene space  that are compatible with existing Recursion pipelines (map building, rescue, target ID/deconvolution, liability detection, biomarker discovery, orthogonal validation, etc)  |
| **Inference**: Experimental metadata and perturbation information: compound representations  (structure, MolGPS) and dose / gene representations (Evo / TxFM) / cellular context, etc, Optionally, control transcriptomic signature.   |  |

### Differentiation:

* We have the largest industry-leading multiomics perturbational datasets, including whole-genome KO \+ double perturbations,  
* Patient-linked cellular and disease context via partnerships  
* In-house compute & ML infra that enables training & deployment  
* Proven ability to operationalize ML into discovery decisions (Centaur, Boltz)  
* Simulations are directly compatible with our existing assays & pipeline, enabling rapid validation & deployment. 

### Key Outcomes (How will we know):

#### Hooke Accelerates Confident, Earlier Decision-Making (Programs & Partnerships)

Hooke simulations reliably substitute for selected wet-lab experiments across novel cellular contexts and perturbations, without degrading therapeutic insight quality.

**Success Metrics:**

- ≥70% alignment between Hooke-driven vs. experimentally driven decisions (target, hit, mechanism) in retrospective evaluation as assessed by program teams  
- ≥30% reduction in time/number of new experiments to reach target, hit, or mechanistic conclusions

**Go / No Go (2026):** If above thresholds are not met, reposition Hooke as a decision-augmentation tool rather than an experimental substitute.

#### Hooke-Based Workflows Are Adopted With Measurable Pipeline Impact

Hooke is embedded into nomination workflows (from hypothesis generation to  virtual phenotypic screening and building hit packages) and influences pipeline progression.

**Success Metrics:**

- ≥1 new program nomination materially enabled or accelerated by Hooke  
- ≥2 program teams actively using Hooke in decision workflows  
- improvement in H2L progression rate at constant experimental capacity after wide deployment of Hooke

**Go / No Go (2026):** If no adoption or no new program nomination, narrow scope to targeted use cases (e.g., virtual rescue screens, mechanistic clarification).
