# (90 min) Q5-Q9: What Could Have Gone Better?

**Q5: What moments meaningfully changed the direction, pace, or morale of the program in a negative way?**

**Q6: What work absorbed significant time, energy, or care but didn’t fully materialize or reach impact?**

**Q7: What drained your energy this year?  In other words, what consistently drained your motivation or focus, even when the work itself was interesting?**

**Q8: Where is there unnecessary friction on Hooke? In other words, where do we need to focus our energy to create a faster route to success?**

**Q9: Which conditions for great research and/or engineering were missing or unstable on Hooke?**

*This section of the feedback is deeply substantive and points to a team that feels "stretched and squeezed." There is a high degree of alignment on the structural and cultural friction points that need to be addressed.*

*Here are 11 key themes for the "What Could Have Gone Better", ranked from the most systemic to more specific technical friction*

**(🙉 1\) \- Is sad \- no notes**

**Everyone is trying their best**

### **Primary Themes**

* **Top-Down Pivots vs. False Agency:** A significant morale drain occurred when the team was solicited for ideas (e.g., Brrr games, pitches) only for those ideas to be bypassed by "top-down" or "executive" decisions. This created a sense of false agency where people felt their input didn't actually influence the roadmap.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 6\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 1\)  (🙉 1\)**  
* **The "Explain vs. Predict" Capacity Gap:** There is a strong sentiment that the team was spread too thin by pursuing **Predict** and **Explain** simultaneously. The "Explain" work absorbed massive energy but is perceived to have had less impact, leading to a "bottleneck" at the integration stage.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 6\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 2\) (🙉 2\)**  
* **Isolation / Direction-less ICs:** Several respondents reported feeling isolated or alone in their work. This was attributed to a lack of dynamic leadership; while leaders were praised for acting as ICs during sprints and being highly accessible, sometimes an absence of guidance left some team members feeling they were working in a vacuum.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 7\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 2\) (🙉 2\)**  
* **The Integration Bottleneck:** The current system design places extreme pressure on the integration team. Because every workstream feeds into a central module, it creates a "pass-through" problem where benchmarking requires constant fine-tuning by a single, overwhelmed group.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 7\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 2\) (🙉 0\)**  
* **Complexity Overkill (Premature Optimization):** A recurring critique is that the team built "too complex solutions too early" without enough intermediate feedback. This resulted in long iteration cycles where it was difficult to pinpoint why an integrated system was failing or succeeding.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 6\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 4\) (🙉 1\)**  
* **Fragmented Knowledge & Silos:** Despite being a small team, "silos" have formed. Limited knowledge transfer and a lack of documentation/tested codebases mean that specific people become bottlenecks for certain experiments, and colleagues feel they lack the "bandwidth" to support each other.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 8\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 2\) (🙉 0\)**  
* **Communication Habits & Positive Feedback:** Beyond technical goals, there is a noted "energy drain" regarding daily communication. Respondents missed small, everyday habits of positive feedback and felt that high-stakes "shout-outs" didn't replace the need for consistent, supportive peer-to-peer interaction.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 1\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 0\) (🙉 9\)**  
* **Physical and Social Isolation:** The "empty office" in Montreal and the lack of social connection (beyond work tasks) contributed to a sense of burnout. Flexibility is valued, but the lack of a "vibrant" hub has made the work feel less sustainable for some.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 2\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 0\) (🙉 8\)**

### **Minority Sentiments (Unique Outliers)**

* **GPU and Headcount Constraints:** While most focused on strategy, some specifically noted that a lack of GPU resources and limited headcount were the primary physical "brakes" on their speed.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 0\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 2\) (🙉 7\)**  
* **The "Generalization" Trade-off:** One technical outlier raised concerns that the team may have prioritized "generality" (multimodal/LLM) too early at the expense of "scalability" (methods that can actually handle all of Recursion’s data).  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 3\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 1\) (🙉 3\)**  
* **Publication Strategy Friction:** A few noted that a "restrictive publication strategy" earlier in the year was a source of frustration, even if it has since been addressed.  
  **(🌱 0\) (🛑 0\) (⏭️ 0\) (💬 1\) (🙉 5\)**
